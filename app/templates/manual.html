INTRODUCTION

Getting the Data Right

The Data Quality Assessment (DQA) process ensures that all reported health data is accurate, consistent, complete, and reliable. It strengthens the systems that support data reporting and verifies that site-level documentation aligns with what is submitted to national platforms and donors.

DQA also builds the capacity of facility teams to manage data effectively and identify programmatic gaps that require action. This comprehensive approach ensures data integrity from source documentation to final reporting.

CORE OBJECTIVES

Goals of the DQA Process

The goals of the DQA process are to:

  • Identify system issues affecting data quality
  • Validate how facilities aggregate, document, and report data
  • Detect and correct discrepancies between source data and reported figures
  • Improve staff capacity in data management and accurate reporting
  • Highlight program quality issues that require follow-up
  • Strengthen long-term data quality improvement across all sites

THE ACE-5 COMPREHENSIVE DQA PROCESS

Focuses on Four dimensions of data quality

DATA VERIFICATION AND VALIDATION

This uses project standardized Data validation and verification (DVV) template:

Validity

Does the data represent the intended result clearly and adequately?

Availability

Are registers, source documents, and forms available, completely filled, signed and sighted on-site?

Consistency

Is the reported data the same across registers, forms, and reporting platforms?

Integrity

Are documents free from alterations or manipulation?

Data validation and verification compares values across:

  • Source registers
  • Monthly summary forms
  • Electronic Medical Records (LAMISplus)
  • District Health Information System (DPT, MSF, NDARS)
  • Other Patient Management and monitoring tools

Validation involves recounting reported numbers and ensuring they align 100% with documentation across all platforms.

AVAILABILITY CHECKS

Document Presence and Completeness

The following must be verified during availability checks:

  • All required source documents and registers must be physically present
  • They should be complete, clearly filled, and duly signed
  • Signatures and dates must align with reporting periods
  • Missing registers or unsigned sections flag potential data gaps

INTEGRITY CHECKS

Data Authenticity and Trustworthiness

During review, staff look for:

  • Alterations or corrections in summary forms and registers
  • Pages torn out, suspicious overwriting, or inconsistent ink usage
  • Documentation inconsistencies suggesting record tampering

CONSISTENCY CHECKS

Cross-Platform Data Alignment

Randomly select folders/forms (e.g., 5 samples) and confirm that:

  • Unique ID matches across all points of documentation
  • Age and sex are consistently maintained across forms
  • Clinical information such as regimen, test results, and ART status is consistent across tools

TX_NEW AUDIT

New ART Client Verification

PURPOSE

To ensure that all newly initiated ART clients are accurately recorded and reported based on defined audit parameters.

WHAT IS AUDITED

For each new ART client, the following must be verified:

Sex Date of Birth ART Start Date Regimen Refill Month TPT commencement date

SOURCES USED

  • Client folders
  • Client referral forms
  • Pharmacy refill records
  • EMR entries
  • Facility ART registers

CLIENT VERIFICATION AUDIT

Deduplication Accuracy

PURPOSE

To ensure the accuracy of client deduplication outcomes and documentation.

WHAT IS AUDITED

True Duplicates

Clients identified as true duplicates and removed from the index facility.

Invalid Clients

Clients who could not be reached and were classified as "invalid."

VERIFICATION PARAMETERS

  • Final verification status (valid/invalid)
  • Date of verification
  • Documented reason for invalidation
  • Consistency between EMR and verification form
  • Availability of biometric enrollment form in client folder
  • Completely filled and properly signed biometrics enrollment and client-verification forms

SOURCES USED

  • Client Verification outcome forms
  • EMR deduplication outcomes
  • State-level final deduplication files
  • Facility documentation records

REMEDIATION PLAN MONITORING

Tracking completion of Corrective Action Plan

PURPOSE

To confirm that all previously identified data issues have been corrected.

WHAT IS AUDITED

  • TX_NEW client records with issues identified in past assessments
  • Indicators where past discrepancies occurred (e.g., mismatch between MSF and registers)
  • Any variables flagged for correction in earlier remediation plans

AUDIT TRAIL

  • Checks are performed across all platforms (registers, EMR, DHIS, etc.)
  • Corrections must be documented, updated, and validated
  • Facility teams are guided to ensure sustainable fixes, not temporary adjustments

ACTION PLAN DEVELOPMENT

After reviewing findings at each site, a corrective action plan is developed. The plan addresses root causes, assigns responsibilities, sets clear timelines, and focuses on strengthening long-term data quality systems and reporting structures.

COMMON GAPS IDENTIFIED IN DQA PROCESSES

Recurring Issues to Avoid

  • Incomplete reporting in the DQA templates
  • Incorrect domain scoring (e.g., awarding a score for consistency when availability was zero)
  • Partial site reporting or non-reporting
  • Missing documentation, unsigned forms, or improperly filled registers
  • Mismatch between EMR and registers

ONGOING IMPROVEMENT STRATEGY

Continuous Quality Enhancement

The DQA methodology emphasizes continued improvement through:

  • Regular training and orientation for facility teams
  • Consistent correction of discrepancies
  • Routine analysis and feedback sessions
  • Implementation and monitoring of remediation plans
  • Continuous strengthening of data systems
  • Ensuring that documentation practice matches national and program standards
